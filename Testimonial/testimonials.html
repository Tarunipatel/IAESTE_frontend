INCOMING INTERNS

My experience with IAESTE India was a magnificent one. Before reaching Manipal University Jaipur I felt that it would be hard to live in India for two and a half months. However, after meeting IAESTE members and fellow interns, who later became my friends, I realized that two and a half months weren’t enough. Everyone was friendly and cheerful; I have made a lot of unforgettable memories. Especially that of weekends, when we traveled a lot. I visited different Indian cities with my friends. I learned about the religion and history of India. I have visited a lot of countries before, but India is different. I still in my normal life think about India. I feel like India is my second home now. The feeling is hard to describe but everything in India is so pure and real. We are now a big family as IAESTE India LC MUJ Interns 2019. Hopefully, we will meet again. Also, Thanks to IAESTE members for everything. It was relaxing to know if anything goes wrong, IAESTE got our backs.

Cem Ozer

Incoming intern

My stay in India was very informative. It not only gave me the experience of working in my field, but also, a wonderful opportunity to learn about the culture, religion, and traditions of India. India that I’ve read about on the internet or I have seen in the movies or television is nothing like the real one. I saw a few places in Rajasthan and all of them were very different from one another, each of them had something different to offer. I spent an amazing eight weeks with some wonderful people who showed and told me all about their culture and traditions. I learned a lot of things during my stay and I had the opportunity to apply my knowledge in practice. My supervisor was very helpful and open to all my questions and comments. During my stay, I also worked with a few Ph.D. students. It was a wonderful experience in all.

Incoming intern

My project was related to Industry 4.0 or Industrial Internet of Things. It involved optimizing the switching time of Traffic lights. The monitoring of traffic lanes was done using a density sensor. In this project, Raspberry Pi was used as an IoT device and the programming was done in Python. Coming from a more theoretical background the chance to implement my knowledge to solving a real-world problem was a welcomed experience. Personally, I was amazed by the crazy ideas people had and this motivated me to think outside the box and to start the pursuit of new dreams. So, there was settling down period for me. This did not last as much as two days as the Local community and students of the institution were amazing! I got into the groove quickly. I stayed in the school Hostel, so my everyday interactions were not limited to just colleagues. There was lots of food to choose from in the Hostel area, so every day, I explored different Indian dishes. It was awesome! On the weekends we went on trips to see ancient Forts like the Taj Mahal, Amer Fort just to name a few. Pink city (Jaipur) has so many touristy places and it was a joy to see most of them. I got the chance to meet students from all over the world! Our diversity made us respect and love each other more. I got to experience a lot of other cultures through everyday interactions. It is an amazing experience which all should try. During my time in India, I got to appreciate the beauty of Nature. From stunning sunsets to the City of Lakes, to the deserts of Jaisalmer to the mountains of the Himalayas, all were simply beautiful. Seeing the impact technology had on making day to day activities easier inspired a hunger in me to thrive for excellence in my field. Seeing so many different people share a laugh will having dinner forever changed my perspective on what is beautiful. I will be forever grateful for this opportunity.

Augustine Chukwu

OUTGOING INTERNS

Once a year ago someplace you have never been before~ Dalai Lama. For me it was China, I still remember when I was applying for the internship, I was so nervous and pessimistic thinking how will I be selected, there are so many students who are better than me but I think God has already planned everything for you. It was my first international trip and will be the most memorable one, I think. I worked in the Quality Control Department of Mettler Toledo and my mentor Darry was the best. He was so helpful and patient throughout my internship. My task was to develop an Object Tracking System which I completed to some extent with the knowledge which I had and acquired during my internship. I had a wonderful experience in China, learned a lot, made new and good friends from different countries like England, Wales, Germany, Poland, Bolivia, Belgium, Serbia, Hungary, Spain, Colombia, got to know about the amazing culture of China, visited so many spectacular cities, tried the delicious traditional Chinese food, bought lots of souvenirs from every city that I visited, for my family or to keep it as a memory. I got attached to the place and people. This only justifies that I had a great time here. Thanks a lot, MT and IAESTE for accepting my offer and giving me such a golden opportunity. This was my first international trip and I can see a great amount of change in me. I am now more confident, independent, hardworking, can open up more easily, I also developed good communication skills, enhanced my technical skills, and I feel like now I have done something professional. I will cherish all these moments which I had, which MT and IAESTE gave me. Two months spent well. So be a traveler, not a tourist. Try new things, meet new people and look beyond what’s right in front of you. Those are the keys to understanding this amazing world we live in.

Bhanvi Badyal
